LIGHTDARK
BRIGHTPALEDULL
  • We can use light and dark to talk about colours. Black is the darkest colour. White is a very light colour.
  • Pale has a similar meaning to 'light'. Pale colours are close to white.
  • We can use bright to talk about light and colour. A bright light is a strong light. A bright day is a day with a lot of sun.
  • When we use bright to talk about colour, it means a strong colour. For example, orange is bright colour.
  • The oppsite of bright is dull. A dull day is a cloudy day. Brown and grey are dull colours.

Conjunctions BUT and HOWEVER

We use but and however to constrast ideas. However has a similiar meaning to but.

This is how we use them:

EXMAPLES

"Austin loves living in Spain but he would prefer to live in Australia with Molly."

"Austin loves living in Spain. However, he would prefer to live in Australia with Molly."

We use however at the beginning of a sentence, not in the middle.

We normally use but in the middle of a sentence.
